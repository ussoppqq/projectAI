**Ibuprofen (Generic Name) in Indonesia**

**1. Indications:**

Ibuprofen is a nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID) used to relieve:

* Pain (mild to moderate) such as headaches, muscle aches, menstrual cramps, toothaches.
* Fever
* Inflammation associated with various conditions.


**2. Pharmacology:**

Ibuprofen inhibits the production of prostaglandins, chemicals in the body that cause pain, inflammation, and fever.


**3. Dosage & Administration:**

* **Adults:** The usual dose is 200-400mg every 4-6 hours as needed, not exceeding the maximum daily dose as specified on the product label.  This maximum daily dose will vary depending on the specific Indonesian formulation.
* **Children:**  Dosage for children is strictly weight-dependent and must be determined by a doctor or pharmacist.  Never give children ibuprofen without consulting a healthcare professional.  There are specific children's formulations available in Indonesia.


**4. Interaction:**

Ibuprofen can interact with:

* Other NSAIDs: Increased risk of bleeding and stomach ulcers.
* Warfarin and other anticoagulants: Increased bleeding risk.
* Lithium: May increase lithium levels.
* Aspirin: May reduce the effects of aspirin.
* Certain diuretics: May reduce the effectiveness of diuretics.
* ACE inhibitors: May increase the risk of kidney problems.
* Corticosteroids: Increased risk of stomach ulcers and bleeding.
* Methotrexate: May increase methotrexate toxicity.


**5. Side Effects:**

* **Common:** Stomach upset, nausea, heartburn, diarrhea, constipation.
* **Serious (less common but require immediate medical attention):**  Allergic reactions (rash, swelling, difficulty breathing), severe stomach pain, bloody or black stools, persistent vomiting, jaundice (yellowing of skin or eyes), kidney problems, liver problems, heart problems (especially in individuals with pre-existing heart conditions).


**6. Pregnancy & Lactation:**

Ibuprofen should be avoided during the third trimester of pregnancy. Use during the first and second trimesters should be only after consulting with a doctor.  It's also important to discuss its use during breastfeeding with a healthcare professional.


**7. Precautions & Warnings:**

* Not recommended for individuals with a history of stomach ulcers, bleeding disorders, severe liver or kidney disease, or allergies to ibuprofen or other NSAIDs.
* Caution is advised in individuals with asthma, heart failure, high blood pressure, or a history of stroke.
* Elderly individuals are more susceptible to side effects.
* It can increase the risk of cardiovascular events (heart attack, stroke) in some people.


**8. Overdose Effects:**

Ibuprofen overdose can cause nausea, vomiting, stomach pain, drowsiness, dizziness, and in severe cases, seizures, coma, and even death. Immediate medical attention is required in case of an overdose.


**9. Storage Conditions:**

Store ibuprofen in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight and heat. Keep out of reach of children.
