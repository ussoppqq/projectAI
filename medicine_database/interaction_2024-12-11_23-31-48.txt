Panadol's generic name is **paracetamol** (also known as acetaminophen).  The information below pertains to paracetamol in general, and *not specifically* to any particular Panadol formulation available in a non-specified "OTHER" location.  **Always consult a doctor or pharmacist in your specific location for accurate dosage, contraindications, and potential interactions as formulations and regulations vary significantly by country.**


1. **Indications:** Paracetamol is used to relieve mild to moderate pain, such as headache, muscle aches, toothache, backache, and pain associated with the common cold or flu. It's also used to reduce fever.

2. **Pharmacology:** Paracetamol's precise mechanism of action is still not fully understood, but it's believed to inhibit cyclooxygenase (COX) enzymes, primarily in the central nervous system, reducing the production of prostaglandins, which are involved in pain and fever.  Unlike many NSAIDs, it doesn't significantly inhibit COX enzymes in the periphery.

3. **Dosage & Administration:**  **This is crucial -  dosage varies significantly by age, weight, and specific formulation.  Do NOT use this information without consulting a medical professional.**  Generally, for adults, the typical dose is 500mg to 1000mg every 4-6 hours, not exceeding 4000mg in 24 hours.  Children's dosage is strictly weight-based and requires careful calculation according to the instructions on the packaging or as directed by a doctor or pharmacist.

4. **Interaction:** Paracetamol can interact with certain medications, including:
    * **Warfarin:** May increase the risk of bleeding.
    * **Alcohol:** Increases the risk of liver damage, especially with chronic use.
    * **Some anticonvulsants (e.g., phenytoin, carbamazepine):** May increase paracetamol metabolism, potentially reducing its effectiveness.
    * **Certain medications for tuberculosis:**  May lead to increased risk of liver toxicity.  This list is not exhaustive.

5. **Side Effects:** Common side effects are generally rare at recommended doses and can include nausea, vomiting, and abdominal pain.  Serious side effects are uncommon but can include allergic reactions (rash, itching, swelling), liver damage (especially with overdose), and low blood cell counts (rare).

6. **Pregnancy & Lactation:** While generally considered safe in pregnancy and breastfeeding at recommended doses, it's crucial to consult a doctor before using paracetamol during pregnancy or breastfeeding.

7. **Precautions & Warnings:** Paracetamol should be used cautiously in individuals with liver or kidney disease.  It should not be used long-term without medical supervision.  Chronic alcohol misuse significantly increases the risk of liver damage when paracetamol is taken.  Always use the lowest effective dose for the shortest duration.  Some formulations may contain other ingredients (e.g., caffeine) which may cause adverse effects in certain individuals.

8. **Overdose Effects:** Paracetamol overdose can cause serious liver damage, potentially leading to liver failure and death.  Immediate medical attention is crucial in case of an overdose.  Treatment involves administering N-acetylcysteine (NAC), an antidote.

9. **Storage Conditions:** Store paracetamol in a cool, dry place, away from direct sunlight and moisture.  Keep out of reach of children.

10. **Medicine Brand Names:** Panadol is one brand name; others vary widely by region.  Examples include Calpol (UK), Tylenol (US), and many others, using the same active ingredient but potentially with different inactive ingredients or formulations.
